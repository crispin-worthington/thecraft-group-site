About Us

Built by Tradespeople,
for Tradespeople

We started The Craft Group because the skilled trades deserve better — better pay, better tools, and better leadership.

Our Story

It Started with a Simple Question

What would happen if someone built a group of trade service companies that actually put people first?

Not a holding company chasing margins. Not a private equity rollup squeezing every dollar out of acquired businesses. Something different — a group that pays well, invests in technology, and treats skilled tradespeople like the professionals they are.

That question became The Craft Group. Founded by Danny Vass, we started with Craft Electric in Calgary and Brink Landscaping in Vernon, BC. Two companies, two trades, one philosophy: hire great people, pay them well, give them the tools to do amazing work, and use modern technology to handle the rest.

The Vision

"Multiple service businesses that leverage AI and hire great folks for high wages to do amazing work."

— Danny Vass, Founder

Our Mission

Make Skilled Trades the Career of Choice

Too many talented people leave the trades because of low pay, bad management, or dead-end jobs. We're building the alternative — companies where skilled work is valued, compensated, and respected.

For Workers

Competitive wages, clear advancement, modern equipment, and a culture that treats you like an adult. No drama, no games — just good work and fair pay.

For Owners

A respectful exit or partnership. We buy your business, keep your team, protect your brand, and bring the resources to grow what you started.

For Communities

Reliable, high-quality service from local teams who are invested in the communities they serve. Better businesses mean better neighbourhoods.

Leadership

Who's Behind This

DV

Danny Vass

Founder & CEO

Danny started his career in the trades and never forgot what it felt like to work hard for someone who didn't value the work. After years of building and running businesses, he founded The Craft Group to create the kind of companies he wished he'd worked for when he was coming up.

He's hands-on, tech-forward, and relentlessly focused on two things: taking care of the people who do the work, and building businesses that last. Danny splits his time between Calgary and Vernon, working directly with each company's team to make sure the vision stays real.

His approach is simple: find good businesses, keep their people, upgrade their tools, and get out of the way. The skilled trades built this country — Danny believes they deserve leadership that acts like it.
